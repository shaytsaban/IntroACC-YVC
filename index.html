Course Aims

The primary goal of financial accounting is to provide useful financial information for making sound economic decisions, particularly in the context of resource allocation. By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Understand the core principles and conceptual framework of financial accounting.
  • Record business transactions using journal entries and post them to ledger accounts.
  • Prepare and interpret the main financial statements: the Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet), Income Statement, and Statement of Changes in Equity.
  • Analyze financial data to assess a company's performance and financial health.
  • Address accounting issues related to key accounts like receivables, fixed assets, and inventory.

Lecture 1: Basics of Financial Accounting Values

This introductory lecture covers the five fundamental elements of accounting. Understanding these concepts is the first step to reading and preparing financial statements.

  • Assets: Economic resources controlled by the entity (e.g., cash, machinery, inventory) that are expected to provide future benefits.
  • Liabilities: The entity's obligations to other parties (e.g., loans, amounts owed to suppliers) that must be settled in the future.
  • Equity: The residual interest in the assets after deducting liabilities. It represents the ownership's stake in the company (Assets - Liabilities = Equity).
  • Income: Increases in economic benefits, such as revenues from sales or services, which result in an increase in equity.
  • Expenses: Decreases in economic benefits, such as the cost of goods sold or salaries, which result in a decrease in equity.
